4 /******************************************************************************
5  * General Message Writing Routines
6  *
7  * This module handles LibTidy's high level output routines, as well as
8  * provides lookup functions and management for keys used for retrieval
9  * of these messages.
10  *
11  * LibTidy emits two general types of output:
12  *
13  * - Reports, which contain data relating to what Tidy discovered in your
14  * source file, and/or what Tidy did to your source file. In some cases
15  * general information about your source file is emitted as well. Reports
16  * are emitted in the current output buffer, but LibTidy users will probably
17  * prefer to hook into a callback in order to take advantage of the data
18  * that are available in a more flexible way.
19  *
20  * - Dialogue, consisting of general information that's not related to your
21  * source file in particular, is also written to the current output buffer
22  * when appropriate.
23  *
24  * Report information typically takes the form of a warning, an error, info,
25  * etc., and the output routines keep track of the count of these.
26  *
27  * The preferred way of handling Tidy diagnostics output is either
28  * - define a new output sink, or
29  * - use a message filter callback routine.
30  * *
31  * (c) 1998-2017 (W3C) MIT, ERCIM, Keio University, University of
32  * Toronto, HTACG
33  * See tidy.h for the copyright notice.
34  ******************************************************************************/
